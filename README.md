# NIMechE-UI Administrative Coordination System

> Structured for continuity.

A unified administrative platform for meeting management, task tracking, reminder automation, documentation, and executive coordination — built to outlast a single tenure.

Developed as a constitutional implementation framework for the Office of the Assistant General Secretary (AGS), Nigerian Institution of Mechanical Engineers, University of Ibadan.

---

## Overview

The Administrative Coordination System is designed to digitise and structure the operational workflow of the NIMechE-UI secretariat.

The system focuses on continuity, accountability, portability, and long-term administrative stability.

Rather than operating through scattered chats, isolated documents, and temporary coordination methods, this platform centralises the core responsibilities of the AGS office into a single operational workspace.

---

## Five Pillars

### 1. Meeting Scheduling
- Calendar-based meeting management
- Recurring meeting support
- Agenda documentation
- Attendance tracking
- Linked meeting minutes

### 2. Task Tracking
- Kanban workflow boards
- Gantt chart planning
- Assignee management
- Priority and deadline tracking
- Deliverable monitoring

### 3. Reminder Automation
- Due-date reminders
- Recurring alerts
- Follow-up notifications
- Administrative prompts

### 4. Documentation
- Structured digital archiving
- Meeting minutes
- Circulars
- Reports
- Constitutional records
- Searchable document access

### 5. Executive Coordination
- Shared GS & AGS workspace
- Task delegation
- Secretariat-wide visibility
- Comment threads and progress tracking

---

## Constitutionally Grounded

This system directly maps to the constitutional duties of the Office of the Assistant General Secretary within the NIMechE-UI SRC structure.

### Constitutional Alignment
- Assist the General Secretary in official duties
- Maintain the NIMechE-UI secretariat
- Compile and maintain the members database
- Execute delegated administrative responsibilities

This project is therefore not merely a software demonstration, but an operational implementation of existing constitutional responsibilities.
